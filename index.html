Harmonata

A tool for instantaneously exploring the extraordinary geometries of harmony. Inspired by the old harmonograph "toy" but with its essential magic removed.

A old fashioned harmonograph typically consisted of three independant oscillators (pendulums): a rotary (rot) oscillator which moved some paper, and two lateral oscillators (x, y) which controlled a pen. You'd set the pendulums swinging, lower the pen, then wait as the pen traces its path and the swinging motions naturally decay. Now you don't have to wait. Here, modern technology also allows the pen hue to oscillate.

Instructions

The vertical sliders may be used to set the properties of the oscillators. Double-click a slider to return to its default value.

"View Gallery" in the "Share" menu for inspiration.

Sliders

amp

The amplitude of an oscillator. Note the lateral oscillators share an amplitude and decay rate.

decay

The (exponential) decay rate of an oscillator. See above.

freq

Oscillator frequecy, constrained to integer values.

fine

Fine (de-) tuning of the oscillator frequency.

phase

Adjust the starting point of the oscillation.

auto

Automatically progress the phase of the oscillator.

off

Offset of the oscillatior value. Only available for ther hue oscillator.

t0

Starting time of the simulation. All oscillators share the time time controls.

t

Finishing time of the simulation.

dt

Time increment in the simulation - reduce for highest resolution (slow) or increase for potentially pleasing discretization effects.

Buttons

Share

Opens sub menu with "Share Image" and "Download Image" options. "Upload to Gallery" and "View Gallery" allow interaction with a public Spiralator gallery.

Hide

Hide the user interface. Bring it back with a double click.
